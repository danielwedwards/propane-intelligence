Scoring Methodology

Proximity Score

Measures how close a target company's locations are to the current platform (Lampton-Love + acquired companies). Each target location scores points based on its distance to the nearest platform location:

  • 10 pts — within 5 miles
  • 7 pts — within 15 miles
  • 5 pts — within 25 miles
  • 3 pts — within 50 miles
  • 1 pt — within 100 miles

County Score

Evaluates the market quality of each target location by looking up the nearest county's estimated propane consumption (within 25 miles). Each location can earn 0-10 points based on the county's consumption relative to the highest-consuming county in the data set.

Total Score

Sum of Proximity + County scores. Higher scores indicate targets that are both geographically close to the platform and located in attractive propane markets.

Score Color Key

Scores are color-coded to indicate acquisition attractiveness:

  • GREEN (30+) — High priority target. Strong proximity and market overlap.
  • AMBER (10-29) — Medium priority. Moderate strategic fit worth evaluating.
  • GRAY (<10) — Monitor. Limited overlap with current platform.

Data Sources & Methodology

All company intelligence is sourced from multiple public and proprietary channels, cross-referenced for accuracy, and assigned a confidence rating. Below is a complete list of sources used:

SourceData Provided
Company WebsitesHistory, services offered, branch locations, contact info
LinkedInPrimary source for key personnel — current executives, titles, org structure. Employee counts and hiring activity
ZoomInfoEmployee counts, revenue estimates, org charts, contact data
FMCSA / DOT RecordsFleet size, driver counts, safety records (federal filings)
LP Gas MagazineTop 50 retailer rankings, annual gallon volumes, M&A activity
BBB & State FilingsFounding dates, ownership structure, incorporation records
Trade PressBPN, Propane Technology, industry publications, chamber directories
Industry BenchmarksRevenue-per-location (~$2.5-3.5M), employees-per-location (~8-12) estimates for companies without public data

Key Personnel Note: LinkedIn is the primary source for executive and management data. Personnel information should be verified periodically as roles change. Names marked with titles like "(Retired)" reflect the most recent available information.

Data Confidence Ratings

⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ (5)Public filings — SEC, annual reports, verified financial statements
⭐⭐⭐⭐ (4)Multiple independent sources — cross-referenced company website, LinkedIn, trade press
⭐⭐⭐ (3)Benchmarks + supporting evidence — industry averages confirmed by partial data
⭐⭐ (2)Benchmarks only — estimates based on location count and industry averages
⭐ (1)Estimates — limited public data, rough approximations
